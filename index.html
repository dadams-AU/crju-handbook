A Message from the Department

Dear Criminal Justice Major,

Welcome to the Division of Politics, Administration, and Justice at Cal State Fullerton! Many of you have chosen Criminal Justice as a major because of a strong interest in the causes, consequences, and control of crime and in pursuing a criminal justice-related career (e.g., law enforcement, corrections, law, victim services) or in continuing to professional or graduate school. Your aspirations require you to set high expectations for yourself and to embrace the journey as you discover your passion and pursue your dreams! Make the effort to engage with department faculty when you have questions about course content or your path to graduation. Also, give yourself the best opportunity to graduate and to reach your career goals by making good choices about how you spend your time, engaging in meaningful internship, research, and volunteer opportunities related to your career, and taking advantage of the resources that are here for you at CSUF.

This handbook is intended to help you navigate the requirements for your bachelor's degree in criminal justice. Please review its contents and keep it for reference throughout your time at CSUF. Please be advised that new requirements for the Criminal Justice major take effect this academic year.

All students are highly encouraged to meet with a major advisor at least once per year, and more often as you get closer to graduation or have any specific questions about your progress or which courses to take. Please bring a current copy of your Titan Degree Audit when attending academic advising sessions. In addition, we recommend that you establish a strong relationship with at least one of your professors so that you have someone who knows you well and can write letters of recommendation when needed.

If you need additional assistance at any time, please stop by the Department office (GH 511) or email pajdiv@fullerton.edu to ask for help. We look forward to meeting you and working with you.

Sincerely,

Sarah Hill, Ph.D. — Professor and Chair, Division of Politics, Administration, and Justice
Alissa R. Ackerman, Ph.D. — Professor and Associate Chair, Department of Criminal Justice

Skills You Will Build in the Major

There are specific recommended skills for individuals working in the criminal justice profession and beyond. Many of those skills are taught within the Criminal Justice major, including:

  • Written and oral communication skills
  • Critical thinking skills
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Skills in research and scientific methodology
  • Ability to stay open-minded and consider diversity in perceptions and perspectives
  • Ability to work cooperatively

Key Policies

Minimum grade requirements

You must earn a C- or better in every required course (catalog year 2019 and later). Catalog years 2013–2018 require a C or better. You also need a minimum 2.0 GPA in all attempted Criminal Justice courses.

D grade rule — core vs. electives

D grades do not count toward the major but do count as free elective units toward your 120-unit total.

Core courses: If you earn a D, you must retake it until you earn at least a C-.

Elective courses: A D does not require a retake, but you still need 15 elective units at C- or higher. Note: if you retake a D-grade course and pass, you will not receive additional units — the credit is already on your TDA.

Course repeat and withdrawal policy

28-unit maximum on total repeated courses.

Grade Forgiveness (up to 16 units): Original grade removed from GPA; only the repeat grade counts. Both stay on transcript. Only applies when both attempts are at CSUF — a class taken at a community college and repeated at CSUF (or vice versa) is not eligible.

Grade Averaged (up to 12 units): Both grades count in your GPA.

Repeats are applied in the order in which they occurred, automatically at the end of the semester after grades post. You cannot repeat a course where you earned a C or better. Maximum 3 total enrollments in any one course.

Check your "Repeated" or "W" units by choosing Withdrawals/Repeats in the dropdown menu of your Student Homepage.

Graduation check — when & how

Apply when you have 85 units — approximately one year before graduation.

How: Portal → Student Homepage → Academic Records → Apply/Pay for Graduation.

Choose your graduation term carefully — an incorrect term can affect advising, enrollment, and financial aid. Consult your advisor or the HSS Graduation Specialist before applying.

The department must approve your check, and you must pay a $115 graduation fee. You need a 2.0 GPA overall and at least 120 total units.

Walking at commencement: to walk in the May ceremonies, you must have graduated the previous Fall or be graduating in Spring or Summer of that calendar year.

Academic notice — what happens

When your CSUF or cumulative GPA falls below 2.0, you are placed on academic notice. The College of HSS places a hold on your account. You must attend an Academic Notice Information Session and meet with the HSS Student Success Team to release it.

Research & Experiential Opportunities

Your education doesn't end in the classroom.

Research Courses

CRJU 499 — Independent Study (1–3 units, up to 6 total): Work one-on-one with a professor on your own project or assist with faculty research. Talk to your favorite professor — research interests are listed in the Faculty Roster below.

CRJU 491 — Applied Policy Research (3 units, permit only): Small-group, real-world policy research on questions proposed by community members, government leaders, and nonprofits. Offered every 2–3 years. Contact Dr. Phil Kopp or Dr. Christie Gardiner (cgardiner@fullerton.edu).

Service-learning classes: CRJU 402 (The Criminal Court Experience) meets weekly at a courthouse to observe the criminal courtroom in action and shadow a member of the California judiciary. CRJU 415 (Policing the City) critically examines methods used by police to control crime in inner-city areas; taught every 2–3 years.

Internships — CRJU 495 & CRJU 492

Start early! Most CJ internships require background checks — contact sites 3–6 months in advance. Federal agencies: plan 1 year ahead (and make sure your grades are stellar). Domestic violence agencies: plan for a 40-hour summer training.

CRJU 495 — CJ Internship: General CJ agencies and nonprofits. Contact Dr. Christie Gardiner (cgardiner@fullerton.edu; GH-530).

CRJU 492 — Pre-law Externship: Legal field internships for those considering law school. Contact Dr. Rob Castro (rcastro@fullerton.edu; GH-544).

Special Programs

Moot Court: CSUF's team is consistently nationally ranked. Sharpen legal writing, oral advocacy, and critical thinking. Especially valuable for pre-law. Contact Dr. Pam Fiber-Ostrow (pfiber@fullerton.edu; GH-532).

Intelligence Community Scholars Program: Prepares students for national security and intelligence careers through advanced study, mentoring, and networking. Minimum 3.2 GPA, rolling applications. Contact Dr. Valerie O'Regan (voregan@fullerton.edu).

Cal State D.C. Scholars: Earn credit while interning in Washington, D.C. Open to juniors, seniors, and grad students of all majors. Contact Amie Zeoli (amzeoli@fullerton.edu) or visit hss.fullerton.edu/cal_state_dc/.

Study Abroad: CRJU students are highly encouraged to study abroad. Visit HSS Study Abroad or CSUF International.
