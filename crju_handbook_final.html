Key Policies
Minimum grade requirements ▾
You must earn a C- or better in every required course (catalog year 2019 and later). Catalog years 2013–2018 require a C or better. You also need a minimum 2.0 GPA in all attempted Criminal Justice courses.
D grade rule — core vs. electives ▾
D grades do not count toward the major but do count as free elective units toward your 120-unit total.

Core courses: If you earn a D, you must retake it until you earn at least a C-.

Elective courses: A D does not require a retake, but you still need 15 elective units at C- or higher. Note: if you retake a D-grade course and pass, you will not receive additional units — the credit is already on your TDA.
Course repeat policy ▾
28-unit maximum on total repeated courses.

Grade Forgiveness (up to 16 units): Original grade removed from GPA; only the repeat grade counts. Both stay on transcript. Only applies when both attempts are at CSUF.

Grade Averaged (up to 12 units): Both grades count in your GPA.

You cannot repeat a course where you earned a C or better. Maximum 3 total enrollments in any one course.
Graduation check — when & how ▾
Apply when you have 85 units — approximately one year before graduation.

How: Portal → Student Homepage → Academic Records → Apply/Pay for Graduation.

Choose your graduation term carefully — an incorrect term can affect advising, enrollment, and financial aid. Consult your advisor or the HSS Graduation Specialist before applying.

The department must approve your check. You must pay a $115 graduation fee. Need a 2.0 GPA overall and at least 120 total units.
Academic notice — what happens ▾
When your CSUF or cumulative GPA falls below 2.0, you are placed on academic notice. The College of HSS places a hold on your account. You must attend an Academic Notice Information Session and meet with the HSS Student Success Team to release it.

How many units per semester? ▾
Spend 2–3 hours studying for every unit of lecture (15 units = 45–60 hours/week on school).

Research & Experiential Opportunities

Your education doesn't end in the classroom.

🔬 Research Courses

CRJU 499 — Independent Study (1–3 units, up to 6 total): Work one-on-one with a professor on your own project or assist with faculty research. Talk to your favorite professor — research interests are listed on the Criminal Justice web page.

CRJU 491 — Applied Policy Research (3 units, permit only): Small-group real-world policy research. Offered every 2–3 years. Contact Dr. Phil Kopp or Dr. Christie Gardiner (cgardiner@fullerton.edu).

💼 Internships — CRJU 495 & CRJU 492
⏰ Start early! Most CJ internships require background checks — contact sites 3–6 months in advance. Federal agencies: plan 1 year ahead. Domestic violence agencies: plan for a 40-hour summer training.

CRJU 495 — CJ Internship: General CJ agencies and nonprofits. Contact Dr. Christie Gardiner (cgardiner@fullerton.edu; GH-530).

CRJU 492 — Pre-law Externship: Legal field internships for those considering law school. Contact Dr. Rob Castro (rcastro@fullerton.edu; GH-544).

🏛️ Special Programs

Moot Court: CSUF's team is consistently nationally ranked. Sharpen legal writing, oral advocacy, and critical thinking. Especially valuable for pre-law. Contact Dr. Pam Fiber-Ostrow (pfiber@fullerton.edu; GH-532).

Intelligence Community Scholars Program: Prepares students for national security/intelligence careers through advanced study, mentoring, and networking. Minimum 3.2 GPA, rolling applications. Contact Dr. Valerie O'Regan (voregan@fullerton.edu).

Cal State D.C. Scholars: Earn credit while interning in Washington, D.C. Open to juniors, seniors, and grad students of all majors. Contact Amie Zeoli (amzeoli@fullerton.edu) or visit hss.fullerton.edu/cal_state_dc/.

Study Abroad: CRJU students are highly encouraged to study abroad. Visit HSS Study Abroad or CSUF International.

🎖️ Student Organizations

Alpha Phi Sigma (APS) — National CJ Honor Society: Requires 12+ CRJU units, 3.0 overall GPA, 3.2 major GPA. Holds speaker events every semester. Contact Dr. Veronica Herrera (veherrera@fullerton.edu; GH-527).

CJSA — Criminal Justice Student Association: Open to all students interested in CJ. Networking, facility tours, forums, volunteer work. Contact Dr. Christie Gardiner (cgardiner@fullerton.edu; GH-530).

CSUF Pre-Law Society: Law school prep, peer networks, faculty connections. Contact Dr. Rob Castro (rcastro@fullerton.edu; UH-544).

💰 Scholarships & Awards

Department awards (applications due in spring): Outstanding Student Leadership & Activities Award · Academic Excellence Award · Hobbs-Dery Award (law-related excellence) · Rose Sullivan & Rebecca Morales Memorial Scholarship ($500 each, for adult re-entry students) · Alumnus of the Year Award.

CSUF Scholarships: Nearly $2 million annually. Most HSS scholarships due in early February. Full details here.

McNair Scholars Program: For first-generation and underrepresented students interested in doctoral study. Must have 59+ units and 3.0 GPA. fullerton.edu/mcnair.
